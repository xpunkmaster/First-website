Clinical microbiology

Microbiology is defined as the study of microorganisms. Clinical microbiology deals with the identification and characterization of infectious microorganisms along with their management i.e. susceptibility/ resistance to respective antimicrobial drugs.

Branches of Clinical Microbiology

Aspects Characterization
Bacteriology Deals with the study of bacteria and its various aspect in diseases and health conditions
Immunology Deals with the study of structure and function of immune system during normal and infectious conditions.
Parasitology Deals with the study of various types of parasites and prasitic diseases
Virology Deals with the study of virus and virus like agents along with their infection and management.
Mycology Deals with vast groups of fungi
